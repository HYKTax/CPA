Overview

The two compliance burdens most likely to bite you.

Payroll and sales tax share a uniquely punishing characteristic: miss a deadline by a single day and the penalties stack up fast. Payroll taxes have "trust fund" liability attached — the IRS can personally assess owners for unpaid withholding, even if the business is a corporation. Sales tax compliance has gotten dramatically worse since the 2018 Wayfair decision, which created economic nexus thresholds in nearly every state, meaning you might owe sales tax in 10 states without ever having a physical location in any of them.

Our payroll practice handles everything: W-2 employees, contractors, bonuses, equity comp, benefits accruals, garnishments, and multi-state withholding. We run payroll through Gusto or Rippling, file federal (Forms 941, 940, W-2, W-3) and state withholding returns, handle all deposits, and issue year-end 1099s to contractors. You approve timesheets; we handle everything else.

Sales tax is harder than payroll because the rules change by state, by city, and by product type. We register you in every state where you have economic nexus, file monthly or quarterly returns per jurisdiction, and remit tax collected. We integrate with Shopify, Amazon, Stripe, and QuickBooks Online to pull sales data automatically. You don't manually do anything after setup.

FAQ

Common questions about payroll and sales tax.

Why not just use Gusto directly and skip the CPA?
You can — and for simple situations (one state, W-2 only, no benefits), that's often fine. Where a CPA adds value: multi-state situations, contractor vs employee classification decisions, equity comp (ISO/RSU/NSO withholding), year-end reconciliation, notices from state agencies, and integration with overall tax planning. Gusto is a great tool; we wield it on your behalf.
What is "economic nexus" for sales tax?
After the 2018 Supreme Court ruling in South Dakota v. Wayfair, states can require out-of-state sellers to collect sales tax once revenue or transaction thresholds are crossed. Most states set thresholds around $100K in revenue OR 200 transactions per year. If your total U.S. sales exceed $500K/year, you likely owe sales tax in 8–15 states — whether or not you've registered there.
What happens if I've been ignoring sales tax?
You have exposure, but most states offer Voluntary Disclosure Agreements (VDAs) that limit back-liability to 3–4 years and often waive penalties. We conduct a nexus study first, then negotiate VDAs before registering. This path is dramatically cheaper than waiting for the state to find you — at which point penalties and interest can equal the underlying tax owed.
Do you handle tipped employees, commissions, and complex payroll?
Yes. Tipped wages with minimum-wage tip-credit calculations, commission-based pay, shift differentials, overtime on multiple rates, equity comp withholding, garnishments, and third-party sick pay — all handled. Restaurants, sales-commission businesses, and tech startups with equity are common clients.
What about benefits — can you handle those too?
We coordinate with your benefits broker but don't sell benefits ourselves (that requires a separate license). We handle the payroll side: health insurance premium deductions, 401(k) contributions and matches, FSA/HSA deductions, and compliance filings (Form 5500 for retirement plans, ACA reporting if applicable).
